Year 3 Module - Grade A

Final Idea

(Fractional) Ownership of Land and Real Estate without the Intermediary of Real Estate Agents

Project Requirements

Smart Contract and Interactive Website

Main Objective

Develop a Proof-of-Concept Website that connects Buyers and Sellers interested in trading Real Estate

Historically, real estate websites and agents focused on listings and facilitating connections between buyers and sellers. Nonetheless, blockchain technology brings fresh avenues for real estate trading, enhancing trading platforms and online marketplaces to offer more comprehensive support for transactions.

Cutting out the Middleman and Improving the Current Ways with Innovative Technology
1

Platform and Marketplace Improvement
This technology allows sellers to tokenize assets, resembling a stock sale, and liquidate the asset through a token sale on the platform. The tokens collected can then be exchanged for fiat currency, granting buyers ownership of a portion of the property.

2

No Intermediaries
According to a Deloitte report, blockchain is poised to change the roles of brokers, lawyers, and banks in real estate transactions. Listings, Payment and Legal Documentation can be automated, potentially eliminating intermediary commissions and fees, and streamlining the process.

3

Improving Liquidity in Real Estate
Real estate is typically considered illiquid due to lengthy sales processes. Crypto can be quickly exchanged for fiat currencies and by tokenizing real estate, sellers can trade portions of their property without waiting for buyers able to afford the entire property, enabling faster access to property value.

4

Decentralisation
Blockchain ensures trust and security by providing transparent and immutable data accessible to all network peers, thereby fostering confidence in transactions and reducing fraud, while recent legislative developments indicate increasing enforceability of smart contracts beyond their technological capabilities.

5

Reducing Costs and Increasing Efficiency
Blockchain's can automate and integrate various processes like inspections, registration, loan fees, and taxes, has the potential to significantly reduce transaction costs associated with real estate, fostering a more transparent, secure, and equitable market accessible to a broader demographic, thus facilitating peer-to-peer real estate transactions on blockchain-powered platforms.

6

Proof-of-Provenance
Past Ownership, Age of Estate, Most recent construction work and Maintenance are some examples of important data valued within Real Estate. With Blockchain's immutable and public smart contracts, any seller/buyer have all the information at their fingertips. Leading to a more informative investment.
